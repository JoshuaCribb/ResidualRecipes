Authentic Vegetable Lo Mien

I love restaurant-style vegetable lo mein because it’s packed with fresh, crisp vegetables and perfectly chewy noodles all tossed in a savory, flavorful sauce. It’s comforting yet light, making it a satisfying meal that feels both wholesome and delicious every time. Lets get into it!

prep time

15 min

cook time

10 min.

serving

4 servings

Tags : Asian

instructions

step 1

Cook the lo mein noodles according to package instructions until just tender (al dente). Drain and toss with a little oil to prevent sticking. Set aside.

step 2

In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin sauce, and sesame oil to make the sauce.

step 3

Heat a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat and add vegetable oil. Add minced garlic and grated ginger, stir-frying for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

step 4

Add the carrots, bell pepper, snap peas, mushrooms, and baby bok choy to the wok. Stir-fry for 3–4 minutes until the vegetables are crisp-tender.

step 5

Add the cooked noodles and sliced green onions to the wok.

step 6

Pour the sauce over the noodles and vegetables. Toss well to combine and heat through, about 2 minutes.

step 7

Add the bean sprouts if using, and toss briefly just to warm them.

step 8

Adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or extra soy sauce as needed. Serve hot, garnished with extra green onions or sesame seeds if desired. ENJOY!

ingredients

8 oz lo mein noodles (or thin egg noodles)

2 tbsp vegetable oil (or peanut oil)

3 garlic cloves, minced

1 tsp fresh ginger, grated

1 medium carrot, julienned

1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced

1 cup snap peas or snow peas

1 cup baby bok choy, chopped

1 cup mushrooms, sliced (shiitake or white)

3 green onions, sliced

½ cup bean sprouts (optional)

2 tbsp soy sauce (light or regular)

1 tbsp oyster sauce (or vegetarian mushroom sauce)

1 tbsp hoisin sauce

1 tsp sesame oil

Salt and pepper, to taste

Optional: chili flakes or sriracha for heat

tools

Wok or large skillet

Cooking spoon or spatula

Pot (for boiling noodles)

Colander or strainer

Measuring spoons

Knife

Cutting board

Mixing bowl (for sauce)
