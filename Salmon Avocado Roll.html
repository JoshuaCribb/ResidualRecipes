Salmon Avocado Roll

I love sushi because it's fresh, simple, and full of flavor without being heavy. The combination of soft rice, delicate fish, and clean ingredients like avocado or cucumber makes it satisfying but light. It's also fun to eat and easy to customize, whether I'm in the mood for something traditional or something more modern. Overall, sushi is one of those foods that always feels clean, balanced, and enjoyable. Join us!

prep time

55-60 min.

cook time

20 min.

serving

2-3 servings

Tags : Asian Food

instructions

step 1

Prepare the Sushi Rice: Rinse the rice: Wash the rice under cold water until the water runs nearly clear. This removes excess starch that makes the rice gummy. Cook the rice: Use a rice cooker or stovetop with 1¼ cups water. Let it steam (covered, off-heat) for 10 minutes after cooking. Make the sushi vinegar (su): In a small pan over low heat, dissolve 2 Tb vinegar, 1 Tb sugar, and ½ tsp salt. Let cool. Season the rice: Transfer cooked rice to a large wooden or non-metal bowl (a hangiri if you have it). Gently fold the vinegar mixture into the rice using a wooden paddle or silicone spatula. Fan the rice while mixing to help it cool and stay shiny. Cover with a damp towel. The rice should be room temperature when you start rolling—not hot or cold.

step 2

Prepare Fillings Cut the salmon into long, thin strips (~¼ inch thick). Slice the avocado just before rolling to prevent browning. Keep the slices about ¼ inch wide. Optionally, squeeze a bit of lemon juice on the avocado to preserve color.

step 3

Set Up Your Rolling Station Wrap your bamboo mat in plastic wrap to prevent sticking. Place a nori sheet, rough side up, shiny side down, on the mat. Wet your fingers in the bowl of water before handling rice—this prevents sticking.

step 4

Set Up Your Rolling Station Wrap your bambooAssemble the Roll Spread rice: Grab a handful of sushi rice (about ¾ cup). Gently spread it across the bottom ¾ of the nori using your fingertips. Press evenly but lightly — don’t mash the rice. Add fillings: Lay a strip of salmon and a strip or two of avocado horizontally across the center of the rice. Optional: Add a very small dab of wasabi before adding the filling.

step 5

Roll the Sushi Lift the bamboo mat edge closest to you and begin to roll it over the filling, using your fingers to keep the filling in place. Tuck and roll: Gently but firmly roll forward while applying even pressure. Don’t roll the mat into the sushi — peel it back as you go. Seal the edge: Use a little water along the top bare edge of nori to seal the roll.

step 6

Cut the Roll Use a sharp chef’s knife or sushi knife. Wet the blade with water (or a damp cloth) between each cut to prevent sticking. Slice each roll into 6–8 even pieces. Use a gentle sawing motion—don’t press straight down or the roll will squish.

step 7 FINISHED!

SERVE: Arrange the slices on a clean plate or sushi tray. Serve with: Soy sauce (for dipping), Pickled ginger (to cleanse the palate), Wasabi (optional—already in the roll for traditionalists), TIPS FOR AUTHENTICITY Keep it simple: no mayo, no spicy sauce, no extra garnishes if aiming for traditional style. Use a small amount of filling so the roll doesn’t burst. Keep your hands and tools clean and slightly damp to prevent sticking. Chill fish properly and use it same day after slicing.

ingredients

1 cup short-grain Japanese rice

1¼ cups cold water

2 Tb rice vinegar

1 Tb granulated sugar

½ tsp kosher salt

6–8 oz sushi-grade salmon, cut into long, thin strips

1 ripe but firm avocado, sliced into thin strips

Optional: A dab of wasabi (between the rice and filling)

4 sheets nori (seaweed), full-size

tools

Bowl of cold water (to wet fingers and knife)

Plastic wrap

Bamboo sushi mat (makisu)

Sharp Knife
