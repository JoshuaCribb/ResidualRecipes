Authentic Beef Tamale

I love traditional beef tamales because they combine tender, slow-cooked shredded beef with soft, flavorful masa dough wrapped in fragrant corn husks. They remind me of family gatherings and cultural traditions, offering a comforting and hearty meal. The rich flavors and unique textures make tamales a special dish that feels like a warm, satisfying celebration in every bite.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Hispanic Food

instructions

step 1

In a large pot, add beef, onion, garlic, dried chilies, cumin, oregano, bay leaves, salt, and pepper. Cover with water or beef broth and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours until the beef is tender and shreds easily.

step 2

Remove the beef from the pot and shred it finely. Discard the onion, garlic, chilies, and bay leaves. Reserve about 3 cups of the cooking broth for preparing the masa dough.

step 3

In a large bowl, mix masa harina, baking powder, and salt. Beat the softened lard or shortening until fluffy, then gradually add the masa mixture alternately with the warm reserved broth until the dough is soft but not sticky.

step 4

Soak the dried corn husks in warm water for 30 minutes to soften. Drain and pat dry before assembling the tamales.

step 5

Spread a thin layer of masa dough (about 2-3 tablespoons) on the smooth side of each corn husk. Add 1-2 tablespoons of shredded beef in the center.

step 6

Fold the sides of the husk toward the center, then fold the bottom up to seal the tamale. Tie with thin strips of husk if desired.

step 7

Arrange the tamales standing up in a large steamer pot. Cover with a damp cloth and steam over boiling water for 1.5 to 2 hours, checking occasionally and adding water as needed.

step 8

The tamales are done when the masa easily separates from the husk. Remove from the steamer and let cool slightly before serving. ENJOY!

ingredients

2 lbs beef chuck roast or brisket

1 onion, quartered

4 garlic cloves

2 dried ancho or guajillo chilies (optional)

1 tsp ground cumin

1 tsp dried oregano

2 bay leaves

Salt and pepper to taste

Water or beef broth (enough to cover meat)

4 cups masa harina (corn flour for tamales)

2 tsp baking powder

1 tsp salt

1 Â½ cups lard or vegetable shortening (softened)

3 to 4 cups warm beef broth or water (reserved from cooking beef)

24 to 30 dried corn husks (soaked in warm water for 30 minutes)
