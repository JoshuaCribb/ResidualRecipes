Szechuan Chicken

I love this restaurant-style Szechuan chicken recipe because it delivers that perfect balance of spicy, savory, and slightly tangy flavors right at home. The bold heat from the chilies and peppercorns, paired with the rich sauce and tender chicken, makes every bite taste just like a dish from a high-quality Chinese restaurant—fresh, exciting, and incredibly satisfying. Time to Eat!

prep time

20min

cook time

15-20min

serving

6 servings

Tags : Asian

instructions

step 1

In a bowl, combine chicken with soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, salt, and cornstarch. Mix well and marinate for 15–20 minutes while you prep the sauce and aromatics.

step 2

In a separate bowl, mix all sauce ingredients—light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, vinegar, hoisin, sugar, sesame oil, water, and cornstarch—until fully combined. Set aside.

step 3

Heat oil in a wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Stir-fry the marinated chicken until browned and cooked through, then remove and set aside.

step 4

In the same pan, add a bit more oil if needed. Stir-fry dried chilies and crushed Sichuan peppercorns for 30 seconds until fragrant. Add garlic, ginger, and optional bell peppers and sauté for 1 minute.

step 5

Return the cooked chicken to the pan. Pour in the prepared sauce and stir-fry everything together for 1–2 minutes, until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the chicken. Add green onions and serve hot.

ingredients

1 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces

1 tbsp soy sauce (for marinade)

1 tbsp Shaoxing wine or dry sherry

1 tsp cornstarch (for marinade)

1/2 tsp salt

3 tbsp vegetable oil (for stir-frying)

8–10 dried red chilies, halved (seeds removed for less heat)

1 tbsp Sichuan peppercorns, lightly crushed

1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced

2 cloves garlic, minced

1/2 red bell pepper, diced (optional)

2 green onions, chopped

1 1/2 tbsp light soy sauce (for sauce)

1 tbsp dark soy sauce

1 tbsp Chinese black vinegar or rice vinegar

1 tbsp hoisin sauce

1 tsp sugar

1/2 tsp sesame oil

2 tbsp water

1 tsp cornstarch (for sauce)
