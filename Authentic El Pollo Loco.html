Authentic Pollo Loco

I love restaurant-style El Pollo Loco because it’s flavorful, juicy, and always hits the spot without being too heavy. The citrus-marinated grilled chicken has that smoky, bold taste that feels both fresh and comforting. Paired with warm rice, creamy queso, and simple sides, it’s a balanced meal that’s quick, satisfying, and never boring. heres how you do it!

prep time

4-24hr (Marinade).

cook time

10-22 min

serving

4 servings

Tags : Hispanic Food

instructions

step 1

IMarinade Instructions: Mix all marinade ingredients in a bowl or ziplock bag. Pound chicken breasts to even thickness for even cooking. Add chicken and marinate at least 4 hours, ideally overnight. Reserve ¼ cup marinade before adding chicken for basting.

step 2

Cooking Methods Grill (Recommended) Preheat grill to medium-high. Remove chicken from marinade, pat lightly dry (not soaked). Grill for 5–6 minutes per side, flipping once, until internal temp reaches 165°F. Baste with reserved marinade during cooking for added flavor. Skillet/Stovetop Heat 1 Tbsp oil in a large skillet over medium-high. Sear chicken breasts for 5–6 min per side until golden and cooked through. Spoon on reserved marinade as a finishing glaze in last 2 minutes. Oven Option Bake at 425°F for 18–22 minutes depending on thickness, basting halfway through.

step 3 FINISHED!

To Serve: Place sliced chicken breast over a bed of Mexican rice. Drizzle warmed queso on top. Garnish with chopped tomatoes, cilantro, or jalapeños if desired. Serve with warm tortillas or a side of beans for full effect. Tips for Success: Do not skip marinating time — citrus tenderizes and infuses flavor. Don’t overcook breasts — use a thermometer for best results. Add smoky flavor with a grill pan if you don’t have an outdoor grill.

ingredients

4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (pounded to even thickness, ~1" thick)

½ cup orange juice

¼ cup pineapple juice (or more orange juice if needed)

¼ cup lime juice (fresh preferred)

2 Tbsp white vinegar

2½ tsp kosher salt

3–4 garlic cloves, minced

1 tsp smoked paprika

½ tsp turmeric (for color)

1 tsp ancho chili powder

½ tsp Mexican oregano

1½ Tbsp neutral oil (like avocado or canola)

tools

Meat mallet or rolling pin (to pound breasts evenly)

Mixing bowl or gallon-size ziplock bag(s) (for marinating)

Measuring Cups and Spoons

Cooking: Grill (gas or charcoal) — preferred for authentic taste

Skillet (cast iron or nonstick) — for stovetop cooking

Oven-safe pan or baking sheet — if using oven method

Tongs (for flipping the chicken)

Instant-read meat thermometer (to ensure doneness — 165°F)
