Tender Beef Brisket

I love tender oven-baked brisket because it’s hearty, flavorful, and comforting. The slow cooking makes the beef incredibly juicy and fall-apart tender, while the savory rub and pan juices create rich, deep flavor in every bite. It’s the kind of meal that feels like home — simple, satisfying, and always worth the wait. Follow Along!

prep time

4-24hr (Marinade)

cook time

4-5hr

serving

7 servings

Tags : American Food

instructions

step 1

Step 1: Season the Brisket: Mix all dry spices in a small bowl. Rub olive oil over brisket, then coat with spice rub all over. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight for best flavor (or at least 2–4 hours).

step 2

Step 2: Bake Low & Slow: Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C). Place brisket fat side up in a roasting pan or baking dish. Add beef broth, Worcestershire, vinegar, and liquid smoke to the pan (not over the brisket). Cover tightly with foil or a lid and bake for 3½ to 4 hours, or until fork-tender.

step 3

Step 3: Uncover & Finish: Uncover the brisket and increase oven temp to 350°F. Baste with pan juices and bake uncovered for another 30–45 minutes to form a nice crust. Internal temp should be at least 195–205°F for fall-apart tenderness.

step 4

Step 4: Rest & Slice: Remove brisket from oven and let rest covered for 20–30 minutes. Slice against the grain for maximum tenderness. Tips for Best Results: Fat side up: Helps keep meat moist. Resting is crucial: Prevents juices from escaping. Slice against the grain: Makes meat more tender.

step 5

Tips for Best Results: Fat side up: Helps keep meat moist. Resting is crucial: Prevents juices from escaping. Slice against the grain: Makes meat more tender.

ingredients

4 lb beef brisket, trimmed (flat cut preferred for even cooking)

2 Tbsp olive oil

2 cups beef broth

1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce

1 Tbsp liquid smoke (optional, for smoky flavor)l

2 Tbsp apple cider vinegar

1 Tbsp kosher salt

2 tsp black pepper

1 Tbsp paprika

1 tsp garlic powder

1 tsp onion powder

1 tsp brown sugar

½ tsp cayenne pepper (optional, for a kick)

tools

Cutting board (preferably for raw meat)

Sharp chef’s knife (for trimming and slicing)

Small bowl (for mixing the spice rub)

Measuring spoons (for spices and liquids)

Tongs or gloves (for handling the raw brisket)

Plastic wrap or a large ziplock bag (for marinating overnight)

Large roasting pan or baking dish with a lid

Heavy-duty aluminum foil (if no lid available)

Oven (preheated to 300°F)

Meat thermometer (instant-read or leave-in)

Basting brush or spoon

Foil or clean dish towel (to cover brisket while resting)

Carving knife (long and sharp for slicing)

Serving platter or cutting board (for slicing/serving)

Optional: Oven-safe wire rack (to slightly elevate the brisket)

Optional: Fat separator (to skim fat from juices)
