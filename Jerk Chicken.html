Jerk Chicken

I love traditional Caribbean jerk chicken because of its incredible blend of bold spices, smoky char, and fiery heat that hits all the right notes. The juicy, tender chicken soaked in a marinade bursting with flavors like allspice, thyme, and Scotch bonnet peppers makes every bite exciting and satisfying. It’s a dish that’s both comforting and vibrant, with a perfect balance of savory, sweet, and spicy elements that keep me coming back for more. Lets Cook!

prep time

4-10hr (Marinade)

cook time

30-40min

serving

5 servings

Tags : Jamaican Food

instructions

step 1

In a blender or food processor, combine scallions, Scotch bonnet peppers, garlic, onion, thyme, allspice, cinnamon, nutmeg, brown sugar, soy sauce, vinegar or lime juice, orange juice, salt, pepper, and oil. Blend until smooth to make the jerk marinade.

step 2

Place chicken pieces in a large bowl or resealable bag. Pour the jerk marinade over the chicken, coating all pieces well. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.

step 3

Preheat grill to medium-high heat. Grill chicken for 25-30 minutes, turning occasionally, until cooked through and charred in spots. Alternatively, bake at 375°F (190°C) for 35-40 minutes, basting occasionally with marinade. ENJOY!

ingredients

3 to 4 lbs chicken pieces (legs, thighs, or cut-up whole chicken)

4 scallions, chopped

2 to 3 Scotch bonnet peppers (seeded for less heat)

3 cloves garlic

1 small onion, chopped

1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves (or 1 tsp dried thyme)

1 tbsp ground allspice

1 tsp ground cinnamon

1 tsp ground nutmeg

2 tbsp brown sugar

2 tbsp soy sauce

2 tbsp white vinegar or lime juice

1/4 cup orange juice

1 tsp salt

1/2 tsp black pepper

2 tbsp olive oil or vegetable oil

tools

Blender or Food Processor

Large Mixing Bowl or Resealable Bag

Grill or Oven

Tongs

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups

Knife and Cutting Board
