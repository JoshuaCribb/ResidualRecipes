Viral Ox Tail Recipe

I love oxtails because they’re the perfect combination of rich flavor, tender meat, and soulful comfort. Slow-cooked until the meat falls off the bone, oxtails soak up spices and seasonings like no other cut, creating a deep, savory gravy that’s unmatched. There’s something incredibly satisfying about the way each bite melts in your mouth—hearty, flavorful, and filled with tradition. Whether paired with rice and peas or just enjoyed on their own, oxtails are more than a meal—they're a connection to culture, warmth, and home. It is impossible to get tired of them...... Here is how we can cook them, the right way:

prep time

30 min. (+2hr Marination)

cook time

2hr 45min

serving

5 servings

Tags : Jamaican Food

instructions

step 1

Prep the Oxtails Pat oxtail dry using paper towels. Season generously with kosher salt and black pepper. Let marinate in the fridge for at least 2 hours (overnight is best). This helps tenderize the meat and infuses flavor before cooking.

step 2

Caramelize the Sugar Heat your Dutch oven or slowcooker/Crockpot/heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Add 3 Tb of light brown sugar directly to the dry pot. Let it melt slowly until it darkens to a rich mahogany color—watch closely to avoid burning. Quickly stir in 1 Tb butter once it bubbles. Add a splash of water (about 2 Tb) to prevent burning and loosen it up.

step 3

Brown the Oxtails Working in batches, add oxtails to the pot, searing each side until deeply brown (~3–4 minutes per side). Remove and set aside on a plate once browned. Don’t overcrowd the pot—this keeps the meat from steaming instead of browning.

step 4

Build the Flavor Base Lower heat to medium. Add half of the chopped onions, half the garlic, half the ginger, and 1/3 of the scallions to the pot. Add the whole Scotch bonnet pepper, thyme sprigs, and allspice berries. Sauté for ~5 minutes, stirring frequently, until soft and fragrant.

step 5

Simmer the Oxtails (Part 1) Return the browned oxtails and juices to the pot. Add just enough water to almost cover the oxtails. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a low simmer. Cover and cook for 1 hour, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.

step 6

6. Flavor Layer (Part 2) After an hour, add the remaining onions, garlic, ginger, and another third of the scallions. Stir in: 2 Tb white sugar 3 Tb soy sauce 1 Tb Worcestershire sauce Stir well and continue to simmer for another hour. The meat should be getting tender—use a fork to test.

step 7

In a small bowl, whisk together: 1 cup of the cooking liquid 3 Tb all-purpose flour Make a smooth slurry and stir it back into the pot. Add 3 Tb ketchup. Let everything simmer for 15–20 minutes, uncovered, to thicken. Stir frequently to prevent sticking at this stage.

step 8

Finish with Butter Beans Gently stir in the butter beans and simmer for 5–10 more minutes. Remove the thyme stems and Scotch bonnet. Taste and adjust seasoning (add salt, pepper, or a splash of soy sauce if needed).

Finished!

Garnish & Serve Garnish with remaining scallions. Serve with: Rice & peas, Steamed white rice, Fried ripe plantains, Sautéed cabbage or callaloo. Pro Tips Flavor layering is key—adding aromatics in two phases deepens taste. For a spicy kick, burst the Scotch bonnet at the end and stir it in. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving to allow flavors to meld. ENJOY!

ingredients

3 Lbs of Oxtails

Kosher Salt & Brown Pepper

3 Tablespoons of Light Brown Sugar

1 Tablespoon of Unsalted Butter

2 Spanish Onions, Chopped

4 Garlic Cloves, Minced

3 Tablespoons of Fresh Ginger, Chopped

1 Whole Scotch Bonnet Pepper

4 Sprigs of Fresh Thyme

12 AllSpice Pimento Berries

1 Bunch Scallions, Chopped (save some for garnish)

2 Tablespoons of White Sugar

3 Tablespoons of Soy Sauce

1 Tablespoon of Worcestershire Sauce

3 TableSpoons of All-Purpose Flour

3 Tablespoons of Ketchup

1 Cup Butter Beans, Cooked or Canned (Rinsed and Drained)
